| "Book a 45-min assessment" | "Book a 45-min discovery call" |
| "Book your 45-min assessment" | "Book your 45-min discovery call" |
| "A 45-min assessment beats a 6-month proposal cycle" | "A 45-min discovery call beats a 6-month proposal cycle" |

## CHANGE 2 — Remove all em dashes (—) site-wide

Em dashes (—) read as agency-speak. Replace with periods, commas, or colons depending on context. Use the rules below.

### Replacement rules

**Rule 1 — When the em dash separates two complete thoughts: replace with a period.**

Before: "Design, build, fit-out, certify — under one contract, on a date your academic calendar can rely on."
After: "Design, build, fit-out, certify. Under one contract. On a date your academic calendar can rely on."

Before: "20–30 vendors across architecture, civil, MEP, finishes, fit-out, and certification — each with their own timeline and their own excuses."
After: "20 to 30 vendors across architecture, civil, MEP, finishes, fit-out, and certification. Each with their own timeline. Each with their own excuses."

**Rule 2 — When the em dash adds a clarifier or aside: replace with a comma or parentheses.**

Before: "We don't outsource — we don't subcontract trades."
After: "We don't outsource. We don't subcontract trades."

Before: "147 compliance items · 1 site survey · 1 timeline locked"
After: (keep middots as is, no change needed)

**Rule 3 — When the em dash sets up a definition or list: replace with a colon.**

Before: "Three stages — one accountability."
After: "Three stages. One accountability."

Before: "Architect hands off to contractor — contractor blames consultant."
After: "Architect hands off to contractor. Contractor blames consultant."

**Rule 4 — En dashes in number ranges (–): replace with the word "to".**

Before: "20–30 vendors"
After: "20 to 30 vendors"

Before: "14–18 months"
After: "14 to 18 months"

Before: "6–8 acres"
After: "6 to 8 acres"

**Rule 5 — Hyphens in compound modifiers stay (no change).**

Keep these as-is: "45-min discovery call", "fit-out", "in-house", "pre-certified-feasible", "single-contract", "K-12", "on-time", "20,000-sqft" etc.
